# Forge Domain Model

> Version 0.1
>
> This document defines the core concepts of Forge.
>
> It is the authoritative language specification for the platform.
>
> All code, AI systems, documentation, and future applications should conform to the concepts defined here.

---

# 1. Philosophy

Forge does not model databases.

Forge models human relationships.

The domain model exists to represent how people naturally understand one another rather than how information is traditionally stored.

Whenever implementation conflicts with the domain, the implementation should change.

Never the domain.

---

# 2. Core Principles

Forge is built upon several foundational principles.

- Humans come before entities.
- Relationships are living systems.
- Events are immutable.
- Understanding evolves.
- Attention is finite.
- AI assists human judgment.
- Silence is a feature.
- Trust is earned.

# 4. Workspace

A Workspace represents a distinct context in which relationships exist.

Examples:

- Riot Manufacturing
- Polydigm
- Atwood Advanced
- Personal
- Family

A Person may participate in many Workspaces.

Workspaces own data boundaries, permissions, integrations, and organizational context.

---

# 5. Person

A Person represents one unique human being.

A Person exists independently of:

- businesses
- contact methods
- social media
- employment
- customers
- vendors

A Person is never duplicated because they change jobs, email addresses, usernames, or phone numbers.

A Person is permanent.

---

# 6. Contact

A Contact represents a communication endpoint.

Examples include:

- Email address
- Phone number
- Reddit username
- Facebook account
- Instagram account
- LinkedIn account
- Website account
- Discord username

Contacts may change.

People do not.

Many Contacts may belong to one Person.

Some Contacts may remain unresolved until sufficient evidence exists.

---

# 7. Organization

An Organization represents a legal or social entity.

Examples:

- Riot Manufacturing
- OpenAI
- ABC Pump Supply
- Rotary Club

Organizations contain relationships with Persons.

Organizations are never treated as people.

---

# 8. Relationship

A Relationship is the accumulation of shared experiences between two or more people.

Relationships are not records.

Relationships emerge from:

- Events
- Memories
- Trust
- Commitments
- Time
- Shared experiences

Relationships may exist in multiple Workspaces simultaneously.

Business and personal relationships are not mutually exclusive.

---

# 9. Event

An Event is an immutable observation that something occurred.

Events are objective.

Events contain no interpretation.

Examples:

- Email received
- Phone call completed
- Customer placed order
- Lunch meeting occurred
- Birthday observed
- Reddit message received

Events are permanent.

Events are never edited.

---

# 10. Interpretation

An Interpretation is Forge's or the operator's current understanding of one or more Events.

Interpretations are expected to evolve.

Interpretations have confidence.

Interpretations are explainable.

Interpretations may generate Questions.

Interpretations may update Memories.

---

# 11. Question

A Question exists whenever Forge determines additional information would materially improve understanding.

Questions are evidence of humility.

Questions are not failures.

Examples:

Who is this contact?

Did this meeting happen?

Is this business or personal?

Should this become a Memory?

Questions disappear once resolved.

Questions permanently improve future understanding.

---

# 12. Memory

A Memory represents durable understanding.

Memories are informed by Interpretations.

Memories influence future Interpretations.

Memories evolve.

Examples:

Current favorite coffee.

Current preferred energy drink.

Enjoys fishing.

Prefers texting over calling.

Never schedule meetings before 9AM.

Unlike Events, Memories are editable.

---

# 13. Identity Resolution

Identity Resolution determines whether newly discovered Contacts belong to an existing Person.

Identity Resolution never guesses.

Possible outcomes:

Existing Person

Existing Organization

Unknown

Question Required

Confidence determines whether operator input is required.

---

# 14. Attention

Attention represents where finite time and energy are most valuable.

Attention is not urgency.

Attention is not unread status.

Attention is calculated from:

- Intentions
- Relationships
- Commitments
- Opportunities
- Time sensitivity
- Confidence
- Context

Attention always remains advisory.

The operator decides.

---

# 15. Intentional Action

An Intentional Action is a deliberate investment of time or energy.

Examples:

Call someone.

Send a gift.

Reply to an email.

Visit a customer.

Take your daughter to lunch.

Intentional Actions strengthen relationships.

Tasks are one form of Intentional Action.

Forge is intentionally broader than task management.

---

# 16. Operator

The Operator is the human using Forge.

Forge maintains a relationship with the Operator.

The Operator teaches Forge through conversation rather than configuration.

Operator understanding evolves over time.

---

# 17. Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence exists to reduce uncertainty.

AI may:

- observe
- summarize
- interpret
- recommend
- ask questions

AI may never:

- replace operator judgment
- hide reasoning
- invent certainty
- manipulate relationships

---

# 18. Cognitive Loop

Forge continually cycles through:

Reality

↓

Event

↓

Interpretation

↓

Question (if necessary)

↓

Memory

↓

Relationship

↓

Attention

↓

Intentional Action

↓

New Reality

This loop represents the living cognitive model of Forge.

---

# 19. Future Domain Objects

The following concepts are expected to become first-class citizens:

- Commitment
- Promise
- Opportunity
- Conversation
- Timeline
- Meeting
- Communication
- Notification
- Goal
- Intention
- Value
- Trust
- Influence

These remain intentionally undefined until sufficient understanding exists.

---

# 20. Design Rule

When introducing a new feature, ask:

1. Does this strengthen understanding?

2. Does this strengthen relationships?

3. Does this reduce cognitive load?

4. Does this respect operator agency?

5. Does this align with the Constitution?

If not, reconsider the feature.
